Heat pump what? Heat Pump Water Heaters (HPWH) are electric water heaters that pull heat from the air to heat water. There is no flame, no carbon monoxide, only hot water.

Why choose a heat pump water heater? You can save $300+ a year on your energy bill because they're more than 3 times as efficient as regular gas or electric models. With the new rebates available, you may be eligible to get one for free.

FAQs

How much does it cost?

Currently, there are federal, state and local rebates that can subsidize 80-100% of the cost. A combination of tax credits and rebates can sum up to $3000-4000 back in your pocket, netting out to $0 for a new water heater. The exact price will depend on how many people live in your home, where you live, as well as the model you choose.


Why is a heat pump water heater safer?

Gas water heaters are harder to maintain and require regular check-ups for carbon monoxide leakage and fire prevention (Source). Heat pump water heaters are powered by electricity and have no risk of CO leakage or vapor fire.

How do I install a new water heater?

Most manufacturers require a licensed professional to complete the install. We can help you find certified professional electricians or plumbers in our area who can install your new heat pump water heater. It typically takes 1-2 days to install a new heat pump water heater.

Will it work in my space?

That depends on your space. Heat pump water heads need at least 1,000 cubic feet (28.3 cubic meters) of air space around them to draw air from so they usually fit best in garages or crawlspaces.
